The Horowitz Curve

The Horowitz Curve (n.) — The empirically observed decline in human task performance as a function of hours awake, plotted against the inconveniently flat line of an agent doing the same job.

Now zoom out to a week.

Agents don't sleep. They don't take weekends. They don't need lunch.

Now zoom out to a year.

Agents don't take vacations. They don't get sick. They don't have slow Mondays.

And it applies to everything.

Code review was just the beginning. The Horowitz Curve shows up wherever humans perform tasks that agents can also do.

It gets worse.

Humans aren't getting smarter. Agents are getting better every year. The window of human superiority isn't just narrow — it's shrinking.
